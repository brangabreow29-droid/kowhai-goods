The Problem with Free Freelance Templates

Most free Excel templates you find online fall into three categories:

  1. Too simple — a single sheet that only tracks invoices, nothing else
  2. Too complex — 20 sheets with macros that break when you breathe on them
  3. Locked — the formulas are protected, so you can't customise anything

The best free option I found was a basic invoice tracker on a Microsoft template gallery. It worked, but it didn't track time, didn't track expenses, and had no dashboard. I was still juggling 3 separate files.

What a Freelance Tracker Actually Needs

Based on running a freelance business for years, here's what you need in one workbook:

  • Clients database — names, contact info, hourly rates
  • Projects tracker — status, budget, hours logged, amount earned
  • Invoices — invoice numbers, payment status (unpaid/paid/overdue)
  • Time tracker — log billable hours by project and task
  • Expense tracker — categorise for tax time
  • Dashboard — total revenue, outstanding invoices, active projects at a glance
